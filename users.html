Prof. Dr.-Ing. Christoph Nytsch-Geusen

My scientific career in a few words:

  • I received my PhD in Engineering at Technische Universität Berlin in 2001.
  • I was a scientific assistant at the Fraunhofer institutes FIRST and FOKUS from 2001 to 2007.
  • A am a university professor since 2007 at UdK Berlin. I hold a chair in “Building Physics and Building Technology at the institute of architecture.
  • I am also working for TU Berlin Campus El Gouna (ZIEG) in Egypt since 2012.

I am a specialist in the simulation of heterogeneous technical systems, scalable modeling and simulation methods for energy systems in rooms, buildings and city quarters. In particular, I am focused on object-oriented, equations-based and interactive simulation methods for energy-efficient building design.

M.Sc. Christoph Banhardt

My scientific career in a few words:

  • I finished my Masters' Degree at TU Berlin in 2013 and continued as a research associate at the TU Berlin.
  • As soon as Corona is over, I will defend my PhD thesis which I wrote about Solar Powered Air Conditioning Systems in the Middle East and North African Region.

My research interest are focused on indoor comfort and human behavior in thermal environments. Further, I like to analyze the ecologigal and societal impacts of technical systems. I am working as Ventilation and Heating development engineer in a Berlin Startup which is focused on fully grid indipendent AND renewable energy supply of single family houses.
